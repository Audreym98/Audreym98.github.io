Unreliability Within Alu Subfamilies

Introduction:

The Alu transposable element family has been shown to have unreliability within its current set of subfamilies during genome annotation. We are currently in the process of developing a method to identify which subfamilies are contributing to this unreliability and when they should be merged. For subfamily pairs that have some amount of unreliability, we compute a confidence value to represent how confident we are that these subfamilies are distinguishable from each other, with a value of 0 being not at all distinguishable.

Hypothesis:

Since some of this unreliability can be explained via point mutations, the younger AluY subfamily will contribute the most to unreliability within the Alu family. Additionally, we expect that subfamily pairs with a low confidence (<=50%) of being distinguishable will be within-type rather than between-type.
